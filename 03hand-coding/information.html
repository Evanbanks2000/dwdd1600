Types of AI: From Narrow to Superintelligent

AI can be classified into three primary categories, each with distinct capabilities and implications.

  1. Narrow or Weak AI: This form of AI specializes in performing specific tasks within a defined domain, such as language translation, image recognition, or virtual assistants like Siri and Alexa. While narrow AI excels in its designated tasks, it lacks the broader cognitive abilities of human intelligence.
  2. General or Strong AI: Representing a more advanced form of AI, general AI demonstrates human-like cognitive capabilities, including understanding, learning, and applying knowledge across diverse tasks. While the achievement of general AI remains theoretical, ongoing research aims to realize machines with human-level intelligence.
  3. Artificial Superintelligence (ASI): ASI denotes a hypothetical scenario wherein AI surpasses human intelligence across all domains, potentially leading to transformative outcomes. Despite its speculative nature, ASI raises ethical and existential questions, prompting ongoing debate among experts.
